Research Interests

Broadly speaking, my research interests lie in the domain of Natural Language Processing (NLP).

More specifically, I am interested in problems related to Natural Language Generation (NLG), where the goal is to generate a sequence given some initial conditions/input e.g French-to-English translation, where one has to generate an English sentence given a French sentence. With the advent of distributed representations and recurrent neural networks, the standard NLG tasks such as translation and summarization have already been conquered (to a considerable extent). Within NLG, I am specifically interested in three settings where the learning process is constrained.

  1. Where there is creative choice involved in the generation process - examples include poems and portmanteaus - creative choice increases the amount of data required for training (since several, diverse alternatives are possible - hence flatter distributions) - while also having lesser data available in practice (there are many books and news articles, but much fewer poems written). Clever pre-training and initialization using related; often non-creative tasks or leveraging language level resources such as dictionaries and word-word networks are some ways of allievating some of these problems.

  2. Where the task requires a model which not only generates answers or predictions, but explains or justifies them - examples include generating stock market predictions from news terms along with explanations for each term;; or answering questions with evidence. Generating explanations often requires constraining the learning process in various ways - and doing so without affecting performance is a challenge. Difficulty of evaluating is a good explanation, along with lack of ground truth are other problems.

  3. Generation tasks for low-resource languages or output domains - one example is transferring the style of a piece of text to another dialect (from a different region; or a different time period) of the same language. Scarcity of direct training data (parallel pairs like in Machine Translation) is a major problem in these settings. Typical ways of allievating this include use of external resources like dictionaries or cognate language resources; intelligent pre-training and initialization on auxiliary tasks; models with better structure/constraints and parameter sharing.


I believe AI agents which can converse in NL, communicate in NL, perform creative linguistic tasks, and explain/justify their actions/choices in NL, are essential to realize an AI-enhanced society.
